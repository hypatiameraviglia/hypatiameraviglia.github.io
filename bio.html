Biography

Hypatia Meraviglia is a planetary systems scientist working on a PhD in geoscience at Arizona State University in sunny Tempe. They specialize in the Saturnian moons (Titan and Enceladus) and ices in the outer Solar System.

Meraviglia is currently working on a PhD in geosciences in Sara Imari Walkers ELIFE lab at Arizona State University, supported in part by the National Science Foundations Graduate Research Fellowship.

Education

In 2023, they graduated magna cum laude from the University of Central Arkansas with a B.A. in linguistics and a B.S. in physics. In 2019, they graduated summa cum laude from the University of Arkansas at Hope-Texarkana with an A.A. in general studies, which they earned in high school.

Research

Meraviglias primary research question is how the different systems of a planetary body (atmospheric chemistry, surface processes, geologic processes, and sometimes subsurface oceanic processes) interlock to produce the complex nevironmental systems we observe.
